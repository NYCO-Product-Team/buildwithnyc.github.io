CUNY Design Fellow

The Service Design Studio is seeking emerging community conscious leaders who are interested in service design, public administration, social services, and/or community organizing for a 3-month fellowship. The Service Design Studio sits within the the Mayor’s Office for Economic Opportunity, and helps further the City’s use of community-centered design and research methods. We partner with City agencies to engage with residents and those who deliver services so that their voices and insights can shape new and existing programs.

Fellows will support our projects in a variety of ways including landscape research to better understand an issue area, supporting design research (co-facilitating workshops, interviews, focus groups, etc), and leading in making materials such as informational one pagers, writing reflective blog posts, and creating worksheets to drive collaboration within our projects.


  Responsibilities

Based on your skills and interests, responsibilities will include:

  • Support in the development of discussion guides, presentation decks, and workshop agendas for collaborative meetings with agency partners, CBO staff, and residents.
  • Co-facilitate workshops, interviews, and focus groups.
  • Co-facilitation and participation in synthesis workshops from interviews and focus groups.
  • Bring your lived experience to collaborative meetings to offer innovative ideas and diversity of perspective./li>
  • Contribute writing to SDS blog, newsletter, and external facing materials.
  • Conduct landscape research and create summaries of insights learned on relevant topics.

  Skills We're Seeking

We understand that candidates may not have all of the skills we are seeking below. We encourage those interested to apply even if they do not meet all of them. We are looking for talented individuals to join our team.

Is currently or intends to participate in the CUNY Accelerate, Complete, and Engagement program (CUNY ACE) in Fall 2023

GPA 3.0+

Strong written communication skills and an interest in creative writing.

Comfortable with public speaking and creating a friendly environment while working with others.

Interest in one or more of the following disciplines: service design, graphic design, UX design, public administration, social services, social work and/or community organizing.

Committed to centering-communities and advocating for low-income individuals and communities of color.

Proven ability to work in a manner that respects differences in race, ethnicity, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, religion, ability and socio-economic circumstance.

Willingness to try on new techniques and approach unfamiliar experiences with curiosity and openness to the process; be willing to step out of ones comfort zone.

Ability to prioritize tasks, strong organizational skills, and work independently.

  Education and Experience

Fellowships are open to post-high school, undergraduate, graduate, or other training program students and recent graduates with a background or interest in storytelling, graphic design, and/or strategic communications.

  Benefits and Compensation

The hourly rate for this position is $22/hr.

Note that all applicants must be authorized to work in the United States. We are unable to provide sponsorship to fellows.
